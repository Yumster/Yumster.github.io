The Social life:

Not alone in my food lust

by Dana Kinita

September 24, 2014

Some people are into cute cat photos, others find dancing toddlers appealing but it's obvious what the common theme on my newsfeed is - food.

I love food. I love its aroma, the presentation and definitely the eating. I could spend hours trawling through photos of edible delicacies salivating over a perfectly cooked steak sliced and gleaming in its juices, chocolate brownies with decadent caramel sauce oozing from its crevices. And bacon ... mmm, bacon.

I don't discriminate, I have a deep affection for cuisines from all over the world. I admire dishes that I can't even pronounce.

And it seems I'm not alone. Food lust is shared among millions who are devout followers and contributors of Facebook pages and Instagram hastags such as Food Porn, #lovefood, Foodspotting, Chowhound and FoodCandy.

An article in the New York Times took note of the growing phenomenon where diners woul d reach for their phones before their cutlery.

According to photo-sharing website Flickr's marketing director, Tara Kirchner, the number of pictures tagged "food" had increased to more than six million in two years. The hashtag #food on Twitter has been used in more than 128 million posts to date.

This admiration doesn't come without its detractors. There would be an equal amount of online users who would subscribe to the mentality of the Facebook page, “Stop taking photos, just eat it”. There's also confusion as to why there's no desire in wanting to learn how these meals are made.

But for me, it's very simple. Sit back, admire and enjoy the visual feast.
